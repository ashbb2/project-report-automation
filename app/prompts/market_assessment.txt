## 4. Market Assessment Introduction
80–120 words: define scope — product boundaries, geography ({market_geography}), target customer segments, what sections 4.1–4.7 will cover.

## 4.1 Industry Analysis and Overview
- Define the market for {product_service} in {market_geography}.
- Describe the value chain and buyer behaviour.
- Explain pricing formation drivers and volatility sources (qualitative unless sourced).
- Summarise key trends: technology, policy, customer procurement shifts.
- If market size or growth figures are available from reputable sources, include them with URLs. Otherwise, state the data limitation clearly.

## 4.2 Market Segmentation
- Segment the market into 4–8 segments relevant to {market_geography}.
- For each segment: buyer type, buying criteria, compliance needs, price sensitivity, and attractiveness rating.
- Recommend target segments aligned to {customer_interface} and {market_orientation} and justify the selection.

## 4.3 Demand Assessment
- Assess demand using a defensible methodology (state which: top-down / bottom-up / surrogate).
- Separate total addressable market from realistically serviceable demand for a new entrant.
- Discuss seasonality and procurement cycles.
- Translate demand into a narrative supporting the project's utilisation ramp-up assumption. Use placeholders if volume figures are not provided by the client.

## 4.4 Demand Drivers
- Identify top demand drivers tied to {target_customer} sectors and macroeconomic indicators.
- Provide leading indicators and how to track them (include URLs where definitions are official).
- List downside triggers and early warning signals relevant to this project.

## 4.5 Supply Assessment
- Describe the supply landscape: supplier categories, regional clusters.
- Discuss effective supply constraints, import role, and lead times. Cite tariff or trade references where relevant.
- Identify supply risks and mitigation options.

## 4.6 Competition Analysis
- Identify competitor archetypes and notable competitors serving {market_geography}. Only name specific competitors if reputable public sources exist.
- Analyse basis of competition and likely incumbent response to a new entrant.
- Conclude with a "where we win / where we are vulnerable" assessment aligned to the project's differentiation strategy.

## 4.7 Demand-Supply Gap and Market Forecast
- Provide a market forecast narrative with base case and downside scenario logic for a 5–7 year horizon.
- State assumptions transparently and cite sources where available.
- Translate forecast into utilisation and pricing implications and list the top 3 sensitivities for financial model testing.

IMPORTANT: Cite URLs wherever market data, statistics, or regulatory references are used. If data is unavailable, say so explicitly rather than inventing numbers.
